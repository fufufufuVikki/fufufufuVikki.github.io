Lovely Nanjing Stories in a Zoo

Ziyi XU

What do you expect most when visiting a zoo? A bear riding a unicycle or a dolphin carrying a ball?

I believe that the happy memories of watching the circus in the zoos will never fade. However, you will no longer find animals used for entertainment in Nanjing Hongshan Forest Zoo since 2011.

It is the first zoo in China to cancel animal performances, which are the financial lifeblood of many zoos.

At the beginning of 2020, the zoo was hit by COVID-19 epidemic. It closed for 51 days, thus suffering losses of more than 30 million yuan（about 3.3 million pounds），equivalent to about 40% of its annual income in previous years.

During the most difficult period, the zoo had only half a million yuan left in its account. 

But now, it has made a remarkable survival.

The man behind the U-turn is Shen Zhijun, 50, an animal welfare advocate and horticulturalist.

In his speech named "A Zoo's Pursuit" in August last year, Shen publicize his decadelong reforms, making Nanjing Hongshan Forest Zoo the latest star on the Internet.

Let the Animals Fall in Love in the Zoo.

01

Originally, Hongshan Zoo was just a "Zoo Park" in a corner of Xuanwu Lake Park, the largest city park in regions south of the Yangtze River .

In 1998, Hongshan Zoo moved from Xuanwu Lake Park to its present location.

Shen took up his post in Hongshan Zoo in 2008. At the age of 37, he was the youngest zoo director in the country at the time.

Since 2009, the staff of the zoo have successively upgraded 19 venues such as the Wolf Pavilion, the Tropical Bird Pavilion, the Monkey Island, the Hornbill Pavilion, the Koala Pavilion and the Meerkat Pavilion.

02

Australia Animals Pavilion was the first to be renovated.

The cassowary lived there is an ancient species that originally inhabited in the bush of northeast Australia.It is a very fierce bird with sharp dagger-like claws. It can easily kick through steel plates up to 5mm thick.

With only about 15,000 left in the wild, it has been listed as endangered by the IUCN (International Union for Conservation of Nature).

Domestic cassowaryies has stopped breeding for many years, and international breeding records are sparse.

When Shen and his staff renovated the pavilion, they incorporated a surrounding grove of metasequoia trees into the whole pavilion, allowing the cassowaries to walk, forage and fall in love freely.

Ater then, the cassowaries began to breed unexpectedly after living in Hongshan more than a decade.

03

In the wild, reproduction of cassowaries follow : a male and a female cassowarries fall in love with each other, and after the female has give a birth, she goes off and continues her courtship, leaving the rest to the father. In a breeding season, a female cassowary can lay two or three broods of eggs.

So in order to strengthen this endangered species, Shen planned to allow the birds to lay more eggs, which they then take out and hatch artificially.

They designed a fake egg with many kinds of sensors inside and a realistic look outside, carrying the function of recording all kinds of data. These data were not available home and abroad at that time.

Based on these data, Shen and his staff successfully carried out artificial incubation. They had hatched 32 cassowarries since 2013 to 2020. They played a very good role in the growth of this endangered species.

Every one of the park's 2,800 animals is treated carefully and nicely like this.

The Worst Time, the Best time

01

In 2020, hit by the Covid-19 epidemic, people have realized that human's life is fragile. Even more vulnerable than humans are the animals in the zoos of the big cities, which are almost forgotten.

Many zoos around the world are struggling to make ends meet because of the lack of visitors. The New Munster Zoo in Germany had no money to buy feed for its animals, so it had to euthanize some of its animals to feed the rest.

The situation at Hongshan Zoo was also very horrible. Shen Zhijun considered for a long time, and finally deferred a part of the bonus of the employees for half a year since he wanted to ensure the supply of animal feed first.

Shen's speech "a zoo's pursit", Aug 2020

02

In August 2020, Shen shared to the public the decadelong reforms of Nanjing Hongshan Forest Zoo－a series of campaigns that transformed the 65-hectare wildlife park located in hilly forests to Nanjing's north, from a traditional zoo to one which prizes wildlife conservation.

They include a bathing pool and giant shower for elephants, a leafy playground in the orangutan valley, a jungle environment for cassowaries－an endangered, fierce bird from northeastern Australia－to roam about and mate, and a secluded resting place for animals about to give birth.

03

The speech was a big success.

Shen's honest assessment of the zoo's situation quickly resonated with netizens. The talk received more than 1.1 million views in the five days after it was released online.

Public interest growing, the zoo has launched a program for netizens to "adopt" animals. Public funding has further improved the living conditions of animals.

During the 2021 Spring Festival holiday, about 30,000 people visit Hongshan Zoo every day.

The True Mission of a Zoo

Nanjing Hongshan Forest Zoo's poster calling for the protection of the Neophocaena aslaeorlentails

Shen has attributed the success of his speech to the fact it has helped the public learn what the "true mission" of a zoo should be.

Traditional zoos have existed for many years to entertain visitors through animal performance and other means. But it is high time that they should change for the welfare of the animals.

"A modern zoo needs to educate the public more about the relationship between wildlife and their habitats and understand the importance of the environment for both human and wildlife survival. It should let people have respect for life and nature and encourage a sustainable way of life," he said.
